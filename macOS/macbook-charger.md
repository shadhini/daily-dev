# About MacBook Pro Chargers

3 main parts:
1. the `Power Adapter` -- the heavy charging brick
    - available in various wattages -- 30W, 67W, 70W, 96W, 140W depending on your MacBook Pro size
    - it converts wall voltage into power for your laptop
2. the `AC Wall Adapter/Duckhead`
    - a small two-pronged/three-pronged plastic piece that slides directly into the power adapter to let you plug it into a wall outlet
    - it can be swapped for a World Travel Adapter Kit for international travel
    - to use Apple chargers in Sri Lanka, 
you need a `UK 3-pin ("Type G") duckhead adapter`, which is the standard wall socket for the country
    - Sri Lanka operates on `230V at 50Hz` -- 
so the adapter just passes power safely to Apple's globally rated chargers which support 100V – 240V
    - `Verbatim 3 Port 65W PD 3.0 & QC 3.0 GaN Universal Travel Adaptor`
3. the Charging Cable -- either USB-C or MagSafe 3
    - most recent models connect to the brick using a standard USB-C cable (which detaches so you can swap out frayed wires)
    - the other end plugs into your Mac via USB-C or a magnetic MagSafe 3 cable
